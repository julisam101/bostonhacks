🏀 Boston Tourist Attractions 🏀

Discover the must-see places in Boston

Downtown Boston

  • Boston Common - America's oldest public park, perfect for walking and relaxing
  • Public Garden - Beautiful garden with lagoon and famous "Make Way for Ducklings" statue
  • Freedom Trail - 2.4-mile historic walking trail connecting 16 significant sites
  • Faneuil Hall & Quincy Market - Historic marketplace with shops and restaurants
  • Downtown Crossing - Major shopping district

🚇 Take the Red or Green Line to Park Street

Fenway & Back Bay

  • Fenway Park - Home of the Boston Red Sox, legendary baseball stadium
  • Newbury Street - Upscale shopping and dining district
  • Boston Public Library - Architectural masterpiece and cultural hub
  • Museum of Fine Arts - World-class art museum
  • Copley Square - Beautiful plaza surrounded by historic buildings

🚇 Take the Green Line B or D to Fenway or Copley

Cambridge

  • Harvard Square - Iconic college area with shops, cafes, and street performers
  • Harvard University - Tour the historic Ivy League campus
  • MIT Campus - Scenic walk around world-class engineering school
  • Harvard Yard - Historic center of Harvard University
  • Coop - College bookstore, student shopping destination

🚇 Take the Red Line to Harvard Square

North End & Waterfront

  • New England Aquarium - See marine life in the iconic building
  • Boston Tea Party Ships Museum - Interactive Revolutionary War experience
  • Paul Revere House - Oldest building in downtown Boston
  • Old North Church - Where the "one if by land" signal was given
  • Rose Kennedy Greenway - Beautiful linear park system

🚇 Take the Blue Line to Aquarium

Seaport District

  • Institute of Contemporary Art (ICA) - Modern art museum with harbor views
  • New Balance World Headquarters - Sports and lifestyle experience
  • Harborwalk - Scenic waterfront promenade
  • Seaport Common - Waterfront park for events and gatherings
  • Boston Children's Museum - Interactive fun for families

🚇 Take the Silver Line to Seaport or the Orange/Red Line to South Station

Museum of Science & Charles River

  • Museum of Science - Interactive exhibits and OMNIMAX theater
  • Charles River Esplanade - Perfect for walking, running, and picnicking
  • Boston Science Park - Great for families and outdoor activities
  • Hatch Shell - Outdoor concert venue on the river
  • Arthur Fiedler Footbridge - Iconic footbridge with great views

🚇 Take the Green Line E to Museum of Science

Beacon Hill

  • Boston State House - Historic government building with stunning architecture
  • Acorn Street - Picturesque brick-paved street, one of Boston's most photographed
  • Mount Vernon Street - Beautiful Victorian row houses
  • Charles Street - Charming antique shops and restaurants
  • Boston Athenaeum - Historic library and membership institution

🚇 Take the Red Line to Charles/MGH

Chinatown & Theater District

  • Chinatown Gate - Historic landmark entrance to Boston's Chinatown
  • Boston Theater District - World-class restaurants and entertainment
  • Wang Theatre - Historic theater venue
  • Orpheum Theatre - Concert venue with historical significance
  • Asian Restaurants & Markets - Authentic dining experiences

🚇 Take the Orange Line to Chinatown

Sports & Recreation

  • TD Garden - Home of Boston Celtics & Bruins, iconic arena
  • Boston Sports Museum - Celebrate Boston's legendary teams
  • Fenway Park - Baseball cathedral and historic ballpark
  • Jamaica Pond - Recreation area with trails and boating
  • Franklin Park - Zoo and historic park space

🚇 Take the Green/Orange Line to North Station

💡 Travel Tips

CharlieCard: Get a reloadable card for unlimited transit access - available at any MBTA station

Boston CityPASS: Discounted admission to multiple attractions - available at visitor centers

Best Time to Visit: Spring (April-May) and Fall (September-October) offer perfect weather

Getting Around: Use the T for quick access to all major attractions
