# 🚇 Boston MBTA Transportation Guide

A comprehensive, Celtics-themed website to help navigate Boston's public transportation system and explore tourist attractions.

## Project Overview

This website was created for Daniel Rodriguez to help visitors and residents navigate Boston's MBTA system. It includes:
- **Route Planning** - Find directions between neighborhoods and attractions
- **T Lines Information** - Details on all MBTA subway lines with major stops and fares
- **Tourist Attractions** - Discover museums, historic sites, shopping districts, and more across Boston

## Features

### 🏠 Home Page (mainpage.html)
- Welcome screen with quick tips about Boston transportation
- Navigation to all main features
- Celtics-themed green design

### 🗺️ Route Planner (startpage.html / continuepage.html)
- Select your current location from 12+ Boston neighborhoods
- Choose from 16+ popular destinations
- Get detailed directions with travel times
- Information about MBTA lines to use

### 🚇 T Lines Information (tlines.html)
- Complete information on all Boston MBTA lines:
  - **Red Line** - North-South connection
  - **Blue Line** - Airport connection
  - **Orange Line** - West-Southwest routes
  - **Green Line** - Light rail with 4 branches
  - **Silver Line** - Bus rapid transit
  - **Commuter Rail** - Extended regional service
- Major stations, fares, and highlights for each line
- Interactive cards with hover effects

### 🏀 Tourist Attractions (attractions.html)
Explore attractions by neighborhood:
- **Downtown Boston** - Commons, Public Garden, Freedom Trail
- **Fenway & Back Bay** - Fenway Park, Newbury Street, Museums
- **Cambridge** - Harvard Square, MIT Campus
- **North End & Waterfront** - Aquarium, Historic Sites, Tea Party Museum
- **Seaport District** - Modern museums, Harborwalk
- **Museum of Science** - Interactive exhibits
- **Beacon Hill** - Historic architecture, charming streets
- **Chinatown & Theater District** - Shopping, dining, entertainment
- **Sports & Recreation** - TD Garden, Boston Sports Museum

## How to Use

### Getting Started
1. Open `mainpage.html` in your web browser
2. Click "Find a Route" to start planning your journey

### Planning a Route
1. Select your current location from the dropdown
2. Choose your destination
3. Click "Find My Route!" to see directions
4. Review the transit line(s) and travel time

### Exploring T Lines
1. Click "View T Lines" from the home page
2. Browse information about each MBTA line
3. See major stops, fares, and highlights

### Discovering Attractions
1. Click "Tourist Attractions" from the home page
2. Browse attractions organized by neighborhood
3. Each listing includes nearby MBTA station information

## Travel Tips

- **CharlieCard**: Get a reloadable card for unlimited MBTA travel
- **Boston CityPASS**: Discounted admission to multiple attractions
- **Best Times**: Spring (April-May) and Fall (September-October)
- **Fare**: Standard MBTA fare is $2.40 per trip
